Our Story

Wintria was originally a weekend project built by Lucas Ou-Yang in 18 hours before his finals.

Lucas was sick of how "bland" the flavor of our current today news tasted. He wanted to create a new way for people to interact with and experience their daily news. In January 2013, Wintria's baby alpha build was released online. It was designed as a humble and hardcoded news feeder, only retrieving and aggregating articles from a mere 4 websites. Due to the overwhelming response from reddit, friends, and family, what was originally a hobby escalated quickly to something much more thrilling.

Wintria was refined since its initial release. Nithin Jilla joined Wintria in May 2013, serving as the visionary for the company with Lucas being the visionary for the product. With Nithinâ€™s vision and contribution, Wintria changed from being an aggregator to a search. A search for news that aimed to be the leader for the way we get news surrounding a certain keyword or a topic of our interest. Nithin helped define the vision, timeline, core features and the features of the minimum viable product along with helping to incorporate Wintria as well as raise seed funding to patent and cover all costs.

Wintria launched into its beta testing phase to gather feedback in order to iterate the next version. During this stage, Lucas decided to focus more on finishing school early so that he can focus on entrepreneurial ventures more wholeheartedly after. He also interned at Facebook for more industry exposure. Following a series of events, Nithin & Lucas made the decision to close out wintria in February 2014. It proved to be a great learning experience for both Lucas & Nithin.
